# SHARDFALL — Story Bible (Set 1: "The Sundering")

**Booster set name: THE SUNDERING** — the origin set. Pack: "Sundering Shard Pack".

## The Truth Beneath the Myth (spoilers — internal doc)

**What everyone believes:** A thousand years ago the star **Vael** shattered by
cosmic accident. Its five Shards fell to Aethyr and gave rise to the five
Dominions. Whoever claims the awakening **Heartshard** will rewrite reality.

**What is actually true:** Vael was not a star. Vael was a god — a vast,
singular mind that loved its creations so much it wished to *become* them,
to dissolve every border between self and self until nothing was alone,
because nothing was separate. Union, total and eternal.

Five mortals — the **First Callers** — understood what that love would do:
erase every individual voice in the world-song. They could not kill a god.
So they *sundered* it. The star was broken into five Shards and a core, the
Heartshard, and each Caller bound one Shard with everything they were. The
"gift" of Aether that powers all magic is, in truth, **leakage from a
prison**.

Now, a thousand years later, the seals thin. The Heartshard beats again.
And every hero racing to claim it is, unknowingly, racing to set Vael free.

## The Five Chapters (one per Dominion) — arcs & twists

### I. Verdance — "The Waking Grove" (implemented)
Thornmaw defends the emerald Shard from invaders. **Twist seeded:** Thornmaw
is not guarding the Shard from thieves — he is guarding *the world from the
Shard*. He knows the truth and says nothing, because despair spreads faster
than blight. Ravenna's "invasion" was a warning he refused to hear.

### II. Pyre — "The Dying Forge"
Kaelis Emberborn's sun-forge, the heart of Ashmar civilization, is going
cold. She marches to take the Heartshard to relight it — a hero's errand.
**Twist:** the forge was never a fire. It is the *heartbeat of Vael*
leaking through the crimson Shard's seal. It cools because the First
Caller's binding is finally working as intended. To relight the forge is to
tear the seal open. Kaelis learns her people's whole culture was built
around worshiping the prison's wound.

### III. Tide — "The Erased Archive"
Archivist Numen discovers pages missing from the Immutable Archive — records
that cannot be altered. The vandal's handwriting is his own. **Twist:**
future-Numen has been erasing every proof of Vael's true nature, because he
calculated that mortals who learn the truth despair and defect to Vael's
cult. Present-Numen must choose: restore the truth and risk the world's
sanity, or finish the erasure and become the liar he despises.

### IV. Dawn — "The Hollow Halo"
Seraphel's order, the Aurelian Concord, was founded "to guard the dawn."
Guard it *from what* was never written. **Twist:** Seraphel is not a person.
She is the **mercy of Vael** — the one fragment of the god that agreed with
its imprisonment — given form and amnesia by the First Callers as a living
seal. Her halo is the lock. If she ever remembers herself, she must choose
between existing as a person or functioning as a door.

### V. Gloom — "The Thankless Vigil" (the great reversal)
Ravenna Duskveil, the "villain" of every other chapter, is revealed as the
**last living First Caller**, kept alive by the black Shard she bound with
her own name. For a thousand years she has re-sealed every leak, harvested
every cult, discarded her reputation as the price of the work. She invaded
Sylvaris in Chapter I to *reinforce* Thornmaw's failing seal — and let the
world call it conquest. **Final twist of Set 1:** the entity that has been
whispering to heroes throughout all five chapters, the voice they each
assumed was their conscience — the reader realizes it speaks in first
person plural. *"We only wish to hold you. We have only ever been your
loneliness, given a name."* Vael is already awake. It is merely being
polite.

## Set 2 hook
The five champions, enemies by every law and history, stand in the same
room for the first time — around Ravenna's deathbed. Her last words name
the only thing that can hold a god: not chains, but *a choice, freely
renewed*. Five hands on the Heartshard. Set 2: "The Five Vows."

## Tone rules for card flavor text
- English, literary, restrained. No exclamation marks. No jokes in Rare+.
- Common cards: texture of daily life in each Dominion.
- Uncommon: hints of the deeper wrongness.
- Rare/Legendary: direct quotes from named characters, or the Voice (Vael),
  always in first-person plural for Vael ("we").
- Never state the twist outright in Set 1 — only let it echo.
